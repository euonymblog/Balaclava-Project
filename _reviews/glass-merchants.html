--- layout: item type: review title: Glass Merchants image: /images/glassmerchants.jpg description: Hip breakfasts and decent coffee in a cool warehouse environment. date_published: 11 May 2017 address: 63-65 Nelson Street map: - 'https://www.google.com/maps/embed?pb=!1m18!1m12!1m3!1d3149.6524052964246!2d144.9910525150722!3d-37.86842284494558!2m3!1f0!2f0!3f0!3m2!1i1024!2i768!4f13.1!3m3!1m2!1s0x6ad6684543dbb8f1%3A0x5e00f28d919ef392!2sGlass+Merchants!5e0!3m2!1sen!2sau!4v1494383059804' opening_hours: 7am-4pm website: 'http://www.glassmerchantsbalaclava.com/' stars: - star - star - star breakfast: true dinner: false wifi: false --- This place seems to have had its ups and downs recently. When we first arrived in Balaclava, we came here quite a bit and loved it. There's a particular lamb and couscous salad one lunchtime that still lingers in my memory. But then the French cook left and things weren't the same for quite a while. With other great places opening in the area, it fell right down the list. THen J ended up getting a coffee in there one morning when nothing else was opening and reported something of a return to form. So we decided to give it another go.

The venue – an old glass factory, as the name suggests – has vestiges of its previous life here and there (“that’s an old band-saw”, said J, the former engineer). Tables and booths seem to have been made with the tools lying around, giving it a nice air of DIY meets industrial chic without being too contrived. The coffee was definitely good and they now serve a big mug, which they didn't before. I had the green scrambled eggs, which had chili and feta and were very tasty. J had a special, a croquette with chorizo and a delicious sauce on the side.

The service was friendly, the food was good, the coffee was better and the price was certainly around par for what we had. It's a very pleasant venue, and we're both really pleased that somewhere that had been a bit of a favourite has got its game face back on.
